# Thinkroom Strategy

## Target problem

Founder-writers increasingly delegate substantial creative work to AI, but generation is faster than their ability to internalize, judge, and stand behind it. They need to distinguish more output from real progress and understand what collaborators endorse when work is shared.

## Our approach

Thinkroom is an open-source, agent-native data and UI layer: external agents bring work in and pick up assignments, while the interface puts humans into deliberate modes for reading, judging, suggesting, endorsing, and acting. We believe judgment and taste remain fundamentally human, so Thinkroom optimizes for deeper thinking and grounded progress—not more generation.

## Who it's for

**Primary:** Founder-writers - They're hiring Thinkroom to think more deeply while using AI, keep their brains engaged, and turn generated work into grounded decisions, actions, and progress.

## Key metrics

- **Grounded work rate** - Percentage of active Thinkrooms reaching human endorsement, measured from document and endorsement records.
- **AI-to-progress conversion** - Agent contributions that produce a decision, completed action, or accepted revision, measured from activity events.
- **Progress per generation** - Meaningful progress actions relative to new agent contributions, measured from activity events.
- **Collaborative alignment** - Shared Thinkrooms where participants reach a commonly endorsed state, measured from endorsement records.
- **Felt ownership** - Response to “I understand and stand behind this work,” measured through a lightweight in-product pulse.

## Tracks

### Agent-native work

External agents can contribute work and pick up assignments without Thinkroom running an embedded agent.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Agents advance the work while Thinkroom remains the human-facing judgment layer.

### Deliberate human modes

The interface puts people into the right mental state for reading, judging, suggesting, endorsing, or acting.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Different kinds of thinking need focused interfaces—not one generic chat box.

### Shared alignment and trust

Make provenance, judgment, and endorsement visible when work is shared.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Collaborators need to understand what others genuinely stand behind and where alignment remains unresolved.

## Not working on

- Running an embedded agent or making chat the primary interface.

## Marketing

**One-liner:** Where deeper thinking compounds.

**Key message:** AI can generate the work, but humans must internalize it before applying judgment and taste. Thinkroom keeps the human brain engaged and turns generated material into shared progress.
